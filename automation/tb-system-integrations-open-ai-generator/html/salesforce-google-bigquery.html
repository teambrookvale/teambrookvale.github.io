Salesforce and Google BigQuery: A Match Made in the Cloud

Salesforce and Google BigQuery, both are renowned cloud-based software applications used by corporates. Salesforce is the leading CRM platform in the world, whereas Google BigQuery is a cloud-based data warehousing tool. Although they cater to different segments, their integration can lead to seamless data handling and help organizations to make well-informed decisions.

Integration of the two through API or SDK

There are two primary ways of integrating Salesforce and Google BigQuery, either through an API or SDK. Salesforce provides a REST API that developers can use to communicate with various applications and code. Similarly, Google BigQuery provides SDKs for various programming languages and an API console for development and testing. By integrating the two through API or SDK, their data is transmitted in a format that both systems can read.

Problems their integration solves

Seamless data transfers

Transferring data between two different systems can be a cumbersome task. Integrating Salesforce and Google BigQuery ensures that data is transferred from one platform to another without any issues. The end-users do not have to worry about saving data in CSV and then uploading it to another system. This saves valuable time, and one can focus on more productive tasks than manual data handling.

Accessibility of tools

Salesforce is a CRM platform that helps organizations manage their customer relationship. Data from customer interactions is of utmost importance and needs to be handled carefully. By integrating Salesforce with Google BigQuery's data warehousing capabilities, organizations can store, analyze, and make meaningful insights out of their customer data better. This integration provides a unique experience where one can access Salesforce and Google BigQuery tools from one source.

Improved decision-making capabilities

By integrating Salesforce and Google BigQuery, organizations can make better decisions. Salespeople can see live reporting which displays customer behavior/interaction patterns, while management decisions can be made based on the business's internal data. This integration means that decisions can be taken quickly and confidently, which is invaluable in today's fast-paced world.

Conclusion

Salesforce and Google BigQuery integration is a match made in the cloud. By integrating the two, organizations can save time and improve productivity. It provides various benefits like seamless data transfers, accessibility of tools, and improved decision-making capabilities. With the world becoming more data-driven, organizations cannot afford to manage data manually. An integration of Salesforce and Google BigQuery can provide an effective solution in harnessing the power of data.
