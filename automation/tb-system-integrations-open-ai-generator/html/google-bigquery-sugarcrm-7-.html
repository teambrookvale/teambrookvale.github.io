Google BigQuery and SugarCRM 7+: Integration through API and SDK

When it comes to managing business data, integrating two powerful tools can often lead to better results. This is where Google BigQuery and SugarCRM 7+ come in handy. In this post, we will explore the integration of these two tools through API and SDK.

What is Google BigQuery?

Google BigQuery is a cloud-based data warehousing tool that allows businesses to store and analyze large datasets in near-real time. With BigQuery, businesses can easily access their data without having to worry about maintenance or setup costs.

What is SugarCRM 7+?

SugarCRM 7+ is a customer relationship management tool designed to help businesses enhance their customer experience. With SugarCRM, businesses can easily manage their customer interactions and track their customer journey from lead generation to sales and beyond.

Integration of the two through API or SDK

The integration of Google BigQuery and SugarCRM 7+ can be done through API or SDK. With API, businesses can seamlessly transfer data between the two tools. This enables businesses to get a real-time view of their data without the need for manual data entry. On the other hand, with SDK, developers can create custom apps that integrate the two tools.

Problems their integration solves

Integrating Google BigQuery and SugarCRM 7+ can solve several problems for businesses:

  • Real-time data analysis - with the integration, businesses can get a real-time view of their data, enabling them to make informed decisions faster.
  • Time-saving - businesses can save time by automating data transfer between the two tools instead of manual data entry.
  • Better customer experience - SugarCRM 7+ allows businesses to improve their customer experience by providing a 360-degree view of their customer.

Conclusion

Integrating Google BigQuery and SugarCRM 7+ can greatly benefit businesses by enabling them to get a real-time view of their data, save time, and improve their customer experience. Whether through API or SDK, the integration of these two tools is a wise investment for businesses looking to streamline their processes and achieve better results.
